## Lore
---

The Ironvein Syndicate emerged in the aftermath of the first two years of the Quell Trials, when one truth became undeniable: **Krallax could not be defeated**.

What began as a trial of worth slowly became a sentence. Hope of victory faded, and those trapped within the Desolate Chain abandoned the idea of escape altogether. Instead, they settled. Camps became districts, districts became cities, and with permanence came ambition.

Power struggles erupted across the islands. Dozens claimed authority, but none could hold it — until **High Regent Cealith Veyron** rose on the island of **Varnhal**. From the heights of **Highthrone Bastion**, she gathered the scattered masses and forged a monarchy where chaos once ruled. Her ascent was swift, calculated, and deeply unsettling to those who feared rebellion.

To prevent an uprising against the trials, **Thorne Quell** bound himself politically to Cealith Veyron. Their alliance ensured stability — or at least the appearance of it — and placed Highthrone Bastion under constant scrutiny. The crown existed, but it did not rule freely.

Yet even under monarchy, one domain remained untouched: **trade**.

No laws governed commerce across the Chain. Smuggling flourished. Coin flowed without record. Resources vanished and reappeared under different banners. It was within this vacuum that the Ironvein Syndicate was born.

A man known as **Crane** — devious, patient, and utterly pragmatic — recognized that control did not require crowns or armies. It required **supply**. By aligning Ironvein with Highthrone Bastion’s rule rather than opposing it, Crane ensured protection, legitimacy, and reach. In return, the monarchy gained economic stability it could not enforce alone.

Thus, the Ironvein Syndicate became the unseen backbone of the Desolate Chain’s economy — monopolizing trade, regulating scarcity, and quietly deciding which cities thrived and which starved.
